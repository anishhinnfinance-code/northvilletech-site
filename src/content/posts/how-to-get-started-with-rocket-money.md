## What Is Rocket Money?

Rocket Money (formerly Truebill) is a personal finance app that connects to your bank accounts and automatically identifies your subscriptions, tracks your spending, and helps you build a budget. It's made by Rocket Companies — the same company behind Rocket Mortgage.

The core promise: **most users find $180–$400 in annual savings** just from canceling subscriptions they forgot about.

The free version covers the basics. Premium ($6–$12/month, you choose what you pay) adds a subscription cancellation concierge, unlimited budget categories, bill negotiation, net worth tracking, and more.

## Step 2: Link Your Bank Account

This is the most important step — and the one that unlocks everything.

Once you're in the app, tap **Link Account** and search for your bank. Rocket Money uses Plaid, the same secure connection layer used by Venmo, Robinhood, and most major finance apps. Your bank credentials are encrypted and never stored directly by Rocket Money.

**Which account should you link first?**

Start with whichever checking account your subscriptions and recurring bills come out of. That's where Rocket Money will find the most useful data, the fastest.

After linking, Rocket Money typically takes 1–3 minutes to scan your transaction history and surface your subscriptions. Most people are surprised by what shows up.

---

## Step 3: Review Your Subscriptions

Once your account is linked, navigate to the **Subscriptions** tab. You'll see every recurring charge Rocket Money detected, organized by category.

Go through each one and mark it:

- **Keep** — services you actively use and want to continue
- **Cancel** — ones you want removed
- **Lower** — bills you want negotiated down (Premium feature)

For Premium members, Rocket Money's concierge team handles cancellations on your behalf — no phone calls required. For free users, Rocket Money shows you exactly how to cancel each service yourself.

**Pro tip:** Pay close attention to charges under $10/month. These are the ones people forget most often — a $7.99 streaming service here, a $4.99 cloud storage plan there — and they add up fast.

---

## Step 4: Set Up Your Budget

Navigate to the **Budget** tab and Rocket Money will auto-populate spending categories based on your actual transaction history. You'll see buckets like:

- Food & Dining
- Entertainment
- Shopping
- Transportation
- Bills & Utilities

Adjust the amounts to what you actually want to spend each month. Rocket Money tracks your real-time spending against these targets and sends alerts when you're approaching a limit.

Free users get a basic budget with default categories. Premium unlocks custom categories, which is especially useful if you want to track gig work expenses, side hustle income, or specific savings goals separately.

---

## Step 5: Check Your Credit Score

In the **Credit** tab, Rocket Money shows your free credit score — no credit card required, no hard pull on your credit. It pulls from TransUnion and updates monthly.

You'll also see a breakdown of what's affecting your score: payment history, credit utilization, account age, and more. It's a useful free addition, even if you already use another credit monitoring service.

## Alternatives Worth Considering

Rocket Money is the best app for subscription tracking and bill negotiation, but depending on your goals, other tools may be a better fit.

**[YNAB (You Need a Budget)](https://northvilletech.co/ynab)** is the stronger choice if you want a more intentional, zero-based budgeting system. It takes more effort to set up but tends to produce better results for people who want to fundamentally change their spending habits. YNAB costs $14.99/month but includes a 34-day free trial.

**[Empower](https://northvilletech.co/empower-signup)** is a great free option if you also want investment tracking, retirement planning, and a net worth dashboard alongside your budget. The personal finance tools are free, and it connects to both bank and brokerage accounts.
