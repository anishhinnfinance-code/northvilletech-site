## What Premium adds

Premium unlocks four things the free plan doesn't have:

### 1. One-tap subscription cancellation

On the free plan, Rocket Money shows you your subscriptions but you have to cancel them yourself — logging into each service, navigating their cancellation flow, sometimes calling a number.

On Premium, you tap **Cancel** inside the app and Rocket Money handles it. For most streaming and software services, it's a one-tap process. This is the feature most people upgrade for.

### 2. Concierge cancellation for harder subscriptions

Some subscriptions are designed to be hard to cancel — gym memberships that require written notice, cable services that insist you call, anything with a retention team waiting to counter-offer.

Premium includes a concierge service that handles these. You submit the cancellation request, Rocket Money's team contacts the provider on your behalf, and you get updates on progress. This is what makes the "we'll cancel anything" promise actually credible.

### 3. Bill negotiation

Rocket Money's team will negotiate your recurring bills — phone, internet, cable, insurance — on your behalf. If they succeed, they keep 35–60% of the first year's savings as their fee. If they don't save you money, you pay nothing.

This is a feature that can pay for itself (and then some) on a single negotiation. A $20/month reduction on your internet bill over 12 months is $240 saved — even after Rocket Money takes their cut, you're ahead.

### 4. Premium customer support

Priority access to Rocket Money's support team. Less relevant for most users, but useful if you run into an issue with a cancellation or account linking problem.

## When the free plan is enough

The free plan makes sense if:

**You just want visibility.** If your goal is to see where your money goes — what you spend, what you owe, what your net worth is — the free plan does all of that. You don't need Premium to get a complete financial picture.

**You're comfortable canceling subscriptions yourself.** If you're willing to log into each service and handle the cancellations manually, the free plan gives you the full list. The actual cancellation work is extra steps, but it's doable.

**You don't have bills to negotiate.** Bill negotiation is the other big Premium draw. If you're already on the best rates for your internet, phone, and insurance, there's nothing for Rocket Money to squeeze.

**You've already done a subscription audit recently.** If you cleaned up your subscriptions in the last six months and you're staying on top of what you're paying for, the cancellation feature adds less marginal value.

## When Premium is worth it

Premium pays for itself quickly in specific situations:

**You have forgotten or unwanted subscriptions.** If you haven't done a full audit in the last year, Rocket Money will almost certainly find money you're losing. Being able to cancel those subscriptions in-app — without navigating each company's cancellation maze — is worth $8/month on its own for a few months.

**You want to set it and forget it for a few months.** Run the subscription audit, cancel what you don't want, negotiate a few bills, then downgrade back to free. You don't have to stay on Premium forever.

The most sensible approach for most people:

3. **Upgrade to Premium** if you have subscriptions to cancel, bills to negotiate, or hard-to-cancel memberships

5. **Downgrade back to free** once you've captured the savings and want ongoing tracking without the monthly cost

The free plan handles everything after the cleanup phase. Premium is most valuable as a tool for the cleanup itself.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I cancel Premium and keep my data?**
Yes. If you downgrade from Premium to the free plan, your linked accounts, transaction history, and settings are all retained. You just lose access to the Premium features.

**Does Rocket Money charge a cancellation fee for Premium?**
No. You can cancel your Premium subscription at any time with no fee. Your access continues until the end of your current billing period.

**Is there an annual plan that's cheaper?**
No — Rocket Money only offers monthly billing. The price is whatever you chose on the slider ($7–$14/month), paid month to month.

**What if bill negotiation doesn't save me anything?**
You pay nothing. Rocket Money only takes their 35–60% fee if they successfully lower your bill. No savings, no charge.

**How do I upgrade to Premium?**
In the Rocket Money app, go to your account settings or tap the Premium banner. You'll be shown the price slider and can choose your monthly rate.
