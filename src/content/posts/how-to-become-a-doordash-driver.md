## Requirements to Become a DoorDash Driver

Before you sign up, make sure you meet the basic requirements. DoorDash keeps these pretty accessible — most people qualify without any issues.

**You'll need:**

- **Age:** 18 years or older (in most markets)
- **Vehicle:** A car, scooter, or bicycle depending on your market (car is most common)
- **Driver's license:** A valid driver's license if you're delivering by car or scooter
- **Auto insurance:** Active car insurance in your name (or the vehicle owner's name)
- **Smartphone:** iPhone (iOS 14+) or Android (version 8.0+)
- **Social Security Number:** Required for the background check
- **Bank account:** For direct deposit of your earnings

That's it. No prior experience, no special certifications, no interview. If you check all those boxes, you're ready to apply.

## Step-by-Step: How to Sign Up as a DoorDash Driver

### Step 1: Start Your Application

Head to the DoorDash Dasher sign-up page to begin:

**[Sign up to be a Dasher →](http://northvilletech.co/dasher)**

You'll be asked to enter your first name, last name, email address, phone number, and zip code. Use the zip code for the area where you plan to deliver — this determines your market.

### Step 2: Choose Your Vehicle Type

After entering your basic info, DoorDash will ask what type of vehicle you'll use:

- **Car** — the most common option and available everywhere
- **Bicycle** — available in select dense urban markets
- **Scooter/Motorcycle** — available in some markets

Most people choose car. If you're in a major city and want to deliver on a bike, just check if that option is available for your zip code.

### Step 3: Submit Your Documents

DoorDash will ask you to upload a few documents:

- **Driver's license** (front and back photo)
- **Vehicle insurance card** (if delivering by car)
- **Profile photo** (a clear headshot — no sunglasses)

These are verified during the background check process. Make sure your photos are clear and legible — blurry photos will slow things down.

### Step 4: Consent to a Background Check

DoorDash uses **Checkr** to run a standard background check on all new Dashers. You'll need to consent to this as part of the sign-up process.

The background check looks at:
- Criminal history (past 7 years)
- Driving record (accidents, DUIs, violations)
- Sex offender registry

Checkr will send you an email once the check is complete. More on timeline below.

### Step 5: Download the Dasher App

While you wait for your background check to clear, download the **Dasher app** (separate from the DoorDash customer app):

- [Download for iPhone (App Store)](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/dasher-delivery-driver/id1247998440)
- [Download for Android (Google Play)](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.doordash.dasher)

Go ahead and log in with the account you just created. You'll see a message that your application is pending until the background check clears.

### Step 6: Activate Your Red Card (If Applicable)

Depending on your market, DoorDash may mail you a **Red Card** — a prepaid debit card used to pay for certain restaurant orders. Not all markets require it, and many orders don't use it at all, but it's good to have.

Your Red Card will arrive by mail within 5–7 business days. Once you receive it, activate it inside the Dasher app under **Settings → Red Card**.

## How Long Does the Background Check Take?

For most applicants, the background check through Checkr clears **within 3–5 business days**. In many cases it's even faster — sometimes just 24–48 hours.

You'll get an email from Checkr when it's complete. If you haven't heard back after 7 business days, log into your Checkr candidate portal (the link is in your original email) to check the status.

**Common reasons for delays:**
- Court records taking time to process
- Incomplete or blurry document photos
- High volume of new applicants in your market

Once your background check clears, you'll get a welcome email from DoorDash and you'll be able to start dashing immediately.

## What Happens After Approval?

Once you're approved, here's what to do:

1. **Open the Dasher app** and tap **Dash Now** or **Schedule a Dash** to pick your first shift
2. **Make sure your Red Card is activated** if your market uses one
3. **Review the app layout** — get familiar with how orders appear, how to navigate, and how to mark orders complete
4. **Head to a high-demand area** — look for the heat map in the app showing where orders are clustering

That's it. You're officially a Dasher.

## How Much Can You Earn as a DoorDash Driver?

Earnings vary by market, time of day, and how efficiently you work. Here's a realistic picture:

| Scenario | Hourly Estimate |
|---|---|
| Part-time, casual hours | $12 – $16/hr |
| Dedicated, peak hours | $18 – $25/hr |
| High-demand market, optimized | $25 – $35+/hr |

**Factors that affect your earnings:**
- **Time of day:** Lunch (11am–2pm) and dinner (5pm–9pm) are typically the busiest and most profitable
- **Day of week:** Weekends generally have more volume and better pay
- **Market:** Dense urban areas tend to have more orders and shorter delivery distances
- **Peak Pay:** DoorDash often adds $1–$5 bonuses per delivery during busy periods
- **Challenges:** DoorDash frequently runs Dasher challenges (e.g., complete 20 deliveries, earn a $50 bonus)

On top of base pay and tips, you keep 100% of customer tips — and tips are often $3–$6 per order.

## Tips for Your First Few Deliveries

**1. Start during peak hours**
Your first few deliveries will take longer as you learn the app and the process. Starting during peak hours (lunch or dinner) means you'll get more order volume and can recoup time with higher earnings.

**2. Double-check the order at pickup**
Before you leave a restaurant, confirm every item on the receipt matches what's in the bag. Missing items lead to customer complaints — and you want to keep your completion and satisfaction ratings high.

**3. Use the in-app navigation**
The Dasher app has built-in navigation. Use it for your first several deliveries — it's optimized for delivery routing and includes gate codes and delivery instructions left by customers.

**4. Communicate with customers**
If a restaurant is running late or there's an issue, send a quick text through the app. Customers appreciate communication and it often results in better tips.

**5. Track your mileage**
As an independent contractor, you can deduct business mileage on your taxes. Use an app like Stride (free) to automatically track your miles from day one.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a car to deliver with DoorDash?**
Not necessarily. In some markets, DoorDash allows bicycle and scooter delivery. However, the vast majority of markets require a car. Check your zip code when signing up to see what vehicle types are available.

**Does DoorDash do a hard credit check?**
No. The background check through Checkr is a criminal and driving record check — it does not affect your credit score in any way.

**Can I drive for DoorDash and Uber Eats at the same time?**
Yes. DoorDash does not require exclusivity. Many Dashers "multi-app" — running DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub, or Instacart simultaneously to maximize their earnings.

**What is the DoorDash Red Card?**
The Red Card is a DoorDash-issued prepaid debit card used to pay for orders at restaurants that don't have a tablet or digital integration with DoorDash. You'll use it for a small percentage of your orders.

**How do I get paid?**
DoorDash pays weekly by direct deposit every Monday for the previous week's earnings. You can also use **Fast Pay** ($1.99 fee) to cash out daily once you've completed 25 deliveries and have been a Dasher for 2+ weeks.

**Is DoorDash available in my area?**
DoorDash operates in thousands of cities across the US, Canada, and Australia. The best way to check is to start the sign-up and enter your zip code — it'll tell you immediately if delivery is available there.

**What if my background check doesn't pass?**
If Checkr finds something that disqualifies you, you'll receive an email with details and instructions on how to dispute any inaccurate information. DoorDash follows Fair Chance hiring practices and the type of offense matters — not every past issue automatically disqualifies you.
